About

My name is Ehab Al Khatib. I am currently working toward a PH.D. degree at SMU under the supervision of Professor Yildirim Hurmuzlu. I have had many opportunities to not tackle cutting-edge problems from multidisciplinary perspectives but also sharpen different skills and programming languages. My research has been interdisciplinary, ranging from system modeling, linear/nonlinear controls of mechatronics, embedded and robotic systems, from micro-scale up. I am a Mechanical engineer with a background in linear/nonlinear controls, state estimation, mechatronics, embedded and robotic systems. Whether for work or leisure, I enjoy exploring ideas that enhance people's lives and broaden the realm of possibilities.

In 2014, I graduated with a BSc in Mechanical/Mechatronics Engineering from Jordan University of Science and Technology (JUST), Jordan. Then, I decided to explore the field of Mechatronics and completed my MSc degree in Mechatronics at the American University of Sharjah (AUS), UAE. In 2016, I joined the Mechanical Engineering Ph.D. program at Southern Methodist University (SMU), TX as the last station of my academic journey.

Thus equipped, I interned with Alcon research, where a host of talented and fun-loving engineers are tucked away. Having completed my internship and very close to be graduated, I am again set loose upon the world in search of the next challenge.
